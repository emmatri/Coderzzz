Exercise

There is strong evidence demonstrating the benefit of exercise on your mental health. The release of endorphins can boost mood, energy levels and reduce stress. So why not give it a go?

You do no need to run a marathon to make the most of exercise. Virtually any form of exercise, from aerobics to yoga, can act as a stress reliever. It can be as simple as a gentle walk or stretches. Discover the connection between exercise and stress relief - see below for more ideas and tips!

YOGA

Yoga is an ancient form of exercise that focuses on strength, flexibility and breathing to boost physical and mental wellbeing.Many studies have found that a little yoga in the morning, at night, or even on a lunch break, can minimise stress and increase productivity. It is believed that yoga is so effective for stress relief because, aside from the physical benefits that yoga brings, it encourages a good mood, an increase in mindfulness, and a healthy dose of self-compassion. Give the poses below a try!

CHILD'S POSE

Child's Pose is perfect for when you've had a long, exhausting day and you just want to get comfy and curl up in a ball. This resting position can be held for as short as 30 seconds up to several minutes â€” depending on how long it takes you to relax into the posture and get fully comfortable. It can relieve back and neck pain when the head and torso are supported, calm the brain, and assist in the reduction of stress and fatigue. Close your eyes, breathe, and let all that stress go.

CAT AND COW POSE

Inhale, lift the heart, the head and the tailbone; exhale and draw in the belly as you lift the spine toward the sky; let the head and tailbone drop, releasing the spine and getting all kinds of lovely space in there. Keep going, following the breath, five times or until you feel ready to move on.

DOWNWARD DOG

The downward dog is the great for blood flow. Tuck the toes and on an exhale, come up into downward-facing dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana). Pedal the feet here, stretching the calves, hamstrings, and just letting the back decompress.

High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

HIIT is great for blood flow and creates a real rush of endorphins. It can be as short as 10 minutes to have a real effect. Try the workout below:

  • 3 min fast walk warm up
  • 30 seconds sprint
  • 30 seconds walk x 3
  • 3 min fast walk cool down
